Pythagorean Theorem

States that the square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides. The theorem can be written as an equation relating the lengths of the sides $a$, $b$ and $c$, often called the "Pythagorean equation":

$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$
